Fixed Gear Bikes

track bike

A track bike is the ultra-purist, high-performance blueprint behind the modern fixed gear, engineered exclusively for racing on enclosed velodrome tracks. Stripped of all superfluous components to shave weight and maximize power transfer, a true track bike features a strictly fixed drivetrain with a single gear, zero brakes, and no freewheel mechanism if the rear wheel is spinning, the pedals are spinning.
Its geometry is noticeably aggressive, featuring a steep headtube angle and a high bottom bracket to prevent the pedals from striking the steeply banked track corners.
While modern street "fixies" lean into urban comfort, authentic track bikes are built entirely from ultra-stiff, aerodynamic carbon fiber or oversized aluminum, utilizing deep-section carbon wheels, narrow drop handlebars, and clipless pedals to convert every ounce of a rider's energy into pure, unadulterated speed.
